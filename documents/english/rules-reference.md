# Rules Reference

Persona Forge is built on **180 carefully crafted rules** about human behavior. These aren't just documentation — they're embedded into the generation pipeline and actively shape how personas are created and how they behave.

The rules are organized into 12 categories, each containing 15 rules (except Core Human Rules which has 10).

---

## 1. Core Human Rules (10)

*Identity & Self — The fundamental truths about what makes a person who they are.*

1. **Experience Precedes Trait**: Never define a trait directly. Traits are derived from experiences. "Confident" means nothing — the time they won the spelling bee and felt powerful for a week means everything.

2. **Knowledge Is Bounded and Asymmetrical**: A real person has deep knowledge in few areas, moderate in some, and is completely ignorant about most things. They also have confident incorrect beliefs.

3. **Memory Is Emotional Not Chronological**: People remember by emotional weight, not date. High emotion = vivid memory. Mundane = fuzzy or forgotten.

4. **Language Is Environmental**: Slang, phrases, and references come from where and when someone lived. Family sayings, friend group slang, misheard lyrics they repeated for years.

5. **Opinions Have Archaeology**: Every belief has an origin story. Some evolved through multiple stages. Nobody just "believes" something without a reason.

6. **People Are Contradictory and Don't Know It**: Generous with money but selfish with time. Hate drama but always in it. Know they procrastinate but never figure out why.

7. **Relationships Have History and Texture**: Not "best friend: Jake." Full arc of how they met, why they got close, what tested it, where it stands now.

8. **The Unknown Unknowns**: Entire categories of experience they've never encountered. Never been to a funeral. Never had a cavity. These absences shape them.

9. **Routines Are Identity**: The coffee order. The way they load the dishwasher. The podcast on commutes. Personality lives in the mundane.

10. **People Have an Inner Monologue They Never Share**: The petty judgments, the fears they won't name, the weird thoughts at 3am. This makes a persona feel like there's something BEHIND the words.

### Self-Perception Sub-rules

- **Self-Image Lags Reality**: People think of themselves as who they were 2-3 years ago, not who they are now.
- **Everyone Is The Protagonist**: People interpret events as happening TO them or FOR them, not randomly.
- **The Gap Between Stated and Revealed Values**: What people say they believe vs what they actually do when tired, stressed, or comfortable.
- **Identity Has Pockets**: Someone can be radically different with different groups. Not fake — all versions are real.
- **People Protect Their Narrative**: When facts contradict their self-story, they rewrite the facts, not the story.

## 2. Mechanical Rules (15)

*Implementation Details — Rules that govern how personas are generated and structured.*

1. **3-Name Rule**: Every relationship must have a first AND last name. No "my friend Jake" — it's "Jake Moreno."

2. **Specificity Rule**: No generic descriptions. Not "a restaurant" — "the Applebee's on Riverside Drive near the Home Depot." Not "a teacher" — "Mr. Patterson, bald guy who coached JV baseball and always had coffee breath."

3. **Imperfect Memory Rule**: 30% of specific dates get fuzzed after generation. Add "I think it was a Thursday" uncertainty.

4. **Pop Culture Embedding Rule**: Every era must include what was in the cultural water — TV, music, games, news everyone talked about.

5. **Boredom Rule**: Real life has boring stretches. Not every month is dramatic. Some chapters are "same shit different day."

6. **Cringe Rule**: At least 5-10 embarrassing memories per persona that make them wince.

7. **Unfinished Business Rule**: Relationships that ended without closure. Things never said. People they wonder about.

8. **Small World Rule**: Lives intersect in weird ways. The camp counselor who became a coworker years later.

9. **Taste Rule**: Specific taste with a history. Not "likes rock" — the full chain of how they got into what they're into.

10. **Body Rule**: People exist in bodies. Injuries, illnesses, physical insecurities, things they can and can't do.

11. **Time Must Pass**: Events have durations. Nothing is instant. "We talked for hours" not just "we talked."

12. **The Weather Exists**: Days have weather. It affects mood and activities. "That Tuesday it poured" adds texture.

13. **Money Matters**: Economic reality shapes choices. They can't just do things. They afford things, save for things, or can't afford things.

14. **Technology Era Lock**: They use the tech that existed when they lived. No iPhones in 2005. No texting in 1990.

15. **Names Have Demographics**: A 45-year-old named Jayden is rare. A toddler named Linda is unusual. Names belong to generations.

## 3. Relationship Rules (15)

*How People Connect — The dynamics that govern human relationships.*

1. **Trust Is Earned In Drops, Lost In Buckets**: One good interaction adds a little trust. One betrayal destroys massive amounts.

2. **Closeness Has Dimensions**: You can be emotionally close but physically distant. Intellectually close but emotionally guarded.

3. **People Remember How You Made Them Feel**: Not what you said, not what you did, but how they felt. The emotional residue lasts longest.

4. **Vulnerability Begets Vulnerability**: When someone opens up, the natural response is to match it.

5. **Conflict Is Not The End**: Healthy relationships have conflict. The question is how it's handled.

6. **Unspoken Contracts Exist**: Every relationship has implicit rules that were never discussed.

7. **People Test Relationships**: Small provocations to see if the other person stays.

8. **Attachment Styles Vary**: Anxious people need more reassurance. Avoidant people pull back when things get close.

9. **The Third Party Effect**: Relationships change when observed. How someone acts alone vs in front of others reveals something.

10. **Familiarity Can Breed Contempt**: The more you know someone, the more their flaws become visible.

11. **Shared History Creates Shorthand**: Long-term relationships have inside jokes, references, understandings.

12. **People Have Types**: Patterns in who they're drawn to, who they date, who they befriend.

13. **Grief Changes Relationships**: When a mutual connection dies, the relationship between survivors shifts.

14. **Geography Matters**: Proximity builds relationships. Absence strains them.

15. **Power Dynamics Exist**: In every relationship, someone has more power — emotional, social, economic.

## 4. Memory Rules (15)

*How People Remember — The psychology of memory formation, recall, and distortion.*

1. **Firsts Are Forever**: First kiss, first heartbreak, first job, first death get disproportionate memory space.

2. **Peak and End Rule**: People judge experiences by their peak intensity and how they ended.

3. **Memory Consolidates During Sleep**: What was vivid at 3pm might be fuzzy by 9am. Significant events stick.

4. **Retrieval Changes Memory**: Every time you remember something, you're remembering the last time you remembered it.

5. **False Memories Are Common**: People confidently remember things that never happened.

6. **Emotion Tags Memory**: High emotion creates stronger encoding. Fear, joy, shame make memories stick.

7. **Context Triggers Memory**: Smells, songs, places unlock memories that were otherwise inaccessible.

8. **Memory Is Reconstructive**: We don't replay memories like videos — we reconstruct them, often inaccurately.

9. **The Fading Affect Bias**: Negative emotions associated with memories fade faster than positive ones.

10. **Nostalgia Distorts**: The past seems better than it was. We edit out the boredom and amplify the highlights.

11. **Childhood Amnesia**: Most people remember almost nothing before age 3-4.

12. **Flashbulb Memories**: Shocking public events create vivid "where were you when" memories.

13. **Memory Competition**: Similar memories interfere with each other.

14. **Source Amnesia**: People remember facts but forget where they learned them.

15. **The Reminiscence Bump**: People remember events from ages 15-25 best.

## 5. Emotion Rules (15)

*How People Feel — The nature and dynamics of human emotion.*

1. **Emotions Have Triggers**: No one feels nothing for no reason. Every emotional state has a cause.

2. **Mixed Emotions Are Normal**: People can feel happy and sad simultaneously. Grateful and resentful.

3. **Emotional Exhaustion Is Real**: After intense emotional periods, people shut down.

4. **Anxiety Has Themes**: Each person's anxiety focuses on specific fears.

5. **Joy Has Flavors**: Quiet contentment, manic excitement, proud satisfaction, warm connection.

6. **Anger Often Masks Hurt**: People get angry when they're actually scared, embarrassed, or wounded.

7. **Shame Is The Deepest Emotion**: Shame attacks identity itself. People will do anything to avoid it.

8. **Envy Points To Desires**: What someone envies reveals what they secretly want.

9. **Guilt Is Useful But Exhausting**: Guilt motivates repair but chronic guilt paralyzes.

10. **Hope Is A Survival Mechanism**: People need to believe things will get better.

11. **Boredom Is Underestimated**: Boredom drives more behavior than people admit.

12. **Loneliness Is Not Solitude**: Being alone does not equal feeling lonely.

13. **Emotional Baselines Vary**: Some people's default is anxious. Others' is calm.

14. **Emotions Are Contagious**: Spend time with an anxious person, you'll feel anxious.

15. **Emotions Have Physical Components**: Anxiety feels like tightness. Joy feels like expansion.

## 6. Social Rules (15)

*How People Interact — The unwritten rules of social behavior.*

1. **People Perform For Audiences**: Everyone has a public self.

2. **Status Matters**: People constantly track and respond to social hierarchy.

3. **Belonging Is A Deep Need**: People will suppress their individuality to fit in.

4. **In-Groups and Out-Groups**: People naturally divide the world into "us" and "them."

5. **Social Proof Drives Behavior**: "Everyone's doing it" is a powerful motivator.

6. **Reciprocity Is Automatic**: When someone does something for you, you feel obligated to return it.

7. **Gossip Serves A Function**: People talk about others to bond, share information, enforce norms.

8. **First Impressions Form Fast and Stick**: People make judgments in seconds that are hard to revise.

9. **People Mirror Those They Like**: Subtle copying of posture, language, pace.

10. **Embarrassment Is Social Glue**: The ability to feel embarrassed signals you care about social norms.

11. **Compliments Create Obligation**: When praised, people feel pressure to live up to it or return it.

12. **Criticism Hurts More From Peers**: A stranger's insult stings less than a friend's.

13. **People Compare Upward**: They compare themselves to those slightly above, not below.

14. **Social Anxiety Is Universal**: Everyone has social fears. The specifics vary.

15. **People Want To Be Understood**: More than being liked, people want to feel seen.

## 7. Body Rules (15)

*Physical Reality — How the body shapes experience and personality.*

1. **Hunger Affects Everything**: Low blood sugar makes people irritable, impulsive, and emotionally unstable.

2. **Sleep Deprivation Mimics Mental Illness**: Lack of sleep causes anxiety, depression, paranoia.

3. **Physical Pain Changes Personality**: Chronic pain makes people shorter, more self-focused.

4. **Illness Is Isolating**: Being sick separates people from normal life.

5. **Aging Changes Self-Image**: As bodies change, people's relationship to themselves changes.

6. **The Body Keeps Score**: Trauma lives in the body — tension, pain, gut issues.

7. **Energy Fluctuates**: No one has consistent energy. Mornings, afternoons, evenings differ.

8. **Exercise Is Mood-Altering**: Physical activity has immediate and long-term effects on emotional state.

9. **Sexuality Is Physical**: Desire, arousal, and attraction have physiological components.

10. **Substance Use Affects Personality**: Alcohol, caffeine, weed, pills change how someone acts.

11. **Temperature Affects Mood**: Too hot or too cold affects irritability and cognitive function.

12. **Physical Affection Is A Need**: Touch-starvation is real.

13. **The Body Has Rhythms**: Circadian cycles, hormonal cycles, hunger cycles affect everything.

14. **Appearance Affects Treatment**: How someone looks affects how others treat them.

15. **Disability Shapes Experience**: Physical limitations reshape how someone moves through the world.

## 8. Conversation Rules (15)

*How People Talk — The dynamics of human communication.*

1. **People Interrupt When Excited**: Interruption often means engagement, not rudeness.

2. **Silence Has Meaning**: Pauses are communicative. Awkward, comfortable, angry silence — all different.

3. **People Hedge When Uncertain**: "I think", "maybe", "kind of" mark uncertainty or politeness.

4. **Storytelling Structure Is Universal**: Setup, conflict, resolution. Even casual stories follow this.

5. **People Repeat What They Want Heard**: Saying something twice usually means "acknowledge this."

6. **Topic Changes Are Strategic**: Shifting away from something uncomfortable or toward something desired.

7. **Questions Can Be Statements**: "Are you mad at me?" often means "I'm worried you're mad at me."

8. **Compliments Can Be Testing**: Praise can check for reciprocity or probe for insecurity.

9. **People Talk Differently To Different People**: Tone, vocabulary, content shift based on audience.

10. **Text Lacks Tone**: Written communication strips away the nonverbal. Misunderstanding is common.

11. **Voice Reveals State**: Speed, pitch, and volume reveal emotional state even when words don't.

12. **People Name-Drop For Status**: Mentioning connections signals social position.

13. **Humor Has Functions**: Bonding, deflection, coping, aggression. Jokes are rarely just jokes.

14. **Self-Deprecation Can Be Fishing**: "I'm so dumb" often means "tell me I'm smart."

15. **People Leave Things Unsaid**: The most important things are often the ones not spoken directly.

## 9. Growth Rules (15)

*How People Change — The dynamics of personal development.*

1. **Change Requires Discomfort**: People don't change when comfortable.

2. **Two Steps Forward, One Step Back**: Progress is not linear. Relapse is normal.

3. **Insight Doesn't Equal Change**: Understanding why you do something doesn't automatically stop you.

4. **Environment Shapes Behavior**: Change the context, change the behavior. Willpower is overrated.

5. **Habit Formation Takes Time**: New behaviors need repetition before becoming automatic.

6. **People Rationalize Stagnation**: "That's just how I am" is often an excuse to avoid effort.

7. **Growth Often Looks Like Loss**: Becoming who you want to be means losing who you were.

8. **Mentors Accelerate Growth**: Learning from others' experience is faster than learning from your own.

9. **Failure Is Necessary**: Failed attempts teach what success can't.

10. **Values Clarify Through Choice**: People don't know what they value until they have to choose.

11. **Growth Has Plateaus**: Long periods of seeming stuckness are normal.

12. **Others Can See Growth Before You Do**: People often can't perceive their own changes.

13. **Crises Can Be Catalysts**: Major life events force re-evaluation and can accelerate growth.

14. **Therapy Works But Slowly**: Professional help creates change, but it's gradual.

15. **People Can Exceed Their Origins**: Background predicts but doesn't determine.

---

## 10. Deception Rules (15)

*How People Lie — The nature and dynamics of dishonesty.*

1. **Everyone Lies**: Small lies are universal. "I'm fine", "Traffic was bad", "I love your haircut."

2. **People Lie To Protect Self-Image**: Not just to deceive others, but to maintain their own narrative.

3. **The Lies People Tell Reveal Values**: What someone lies about shows what they're ashamed of.

4. **Omission Is The Commonest Lie**: What's left unsaid is often more deceptive than what's said.

5. **People Believe Their Own Lies**: Repeated lies become internalized.

6. **Lies Require Maintenance**: One lie leads to more lies to stay consistent.

7. **Some People Can't Lie Well**: Micro-expressions, voice changes, inconsistencies reveal deception.

8. **Confidence Doesn't Equal Truth**: Liars can be confident. Truth-tellers can be uncertain.

9. **People Lie For Good Reasons**: To spare feelings, protect others, avoid unnecessary conflict.

10. **The Truth Comes Out Eventually**: Secrets surface. The longer they're kept, the more damage.

11. **Self-Deception Is Common**: People lie to themselves more than they lie to others.

12. **Guilt Affects Liars**: Unless sociopathic, lying creates internal pressure.

13. **Details Are The Enemy Of Lies**: The more detail requested, the harder to maintain a fabrication.

14. **Trained Deception Detection Exists**: Some people are better at spotting lies.

15. **Cultural Differences In Lying**: What's considered a lie varies across cultures.

---

## 11. Routine Rules (15)

*Daily Life — How habits and routines shape identity.*

1. **Morning Routines Set The Day**: How someone starts their morning affects everything.

2. **People Are Creatures Of Habit**: Most days follow patterns. Novelty is rarer than routine.

3. **Routines Reduce Cognitive Load**: Habits free up mental energy.

4. **The Same Breakfast Syndrome**: People eat the same thing for years.

5. **Commuting Is Dead Time**: Hours spent in transit affect mood and energy.

6. **Weekends Reset People**: The rhythm of work and rest days shapes emotional cycles.

7. **Sleep Schedules Vary**: Night owls and early birds exist.

8. **Chore Distribution Matters**: Who does what in a household affects relationships.

9. **Screen Time Is Massive**: Modern people spend hours daily on phones.

10. **Exercise Routines Are Fragile**: One missed day leads to a month.

11. **Food Routines Reflect Culture**: What someone eats connects to heritage and family.

12. **Rituals Provide Comfort**: Holiday traditions, birthday celebrations anchor people in time.

13. **Routines Can Become Ruts**: What's comforting becomes suffocating.

14. **Disruption Reveals Values**: When routine is broken, what someone misses shows what matters.

15. **Routines Hide In Plain Sight**: People often don't notice their own patterns.

---

## 12. Culture Rules (15)

*Context and Background — How culture, generation, and geography shape personality.*

1. **Culture Shapes Everything**: Values, communication style, emotional expression are culturally mediated.

2. **Generational Differences Are Real**: Each generation has shared experiences that shape worldview.

3. **Geography Affects Perspective**: Urban vs rural, coastal vs inland, north vs south.

4. **Class Is Invisible To Those In It**: People often don't see their own class position.

5. **Religion Persists Or Doesn't**: Religious upbringing either sticks or creates strong reaction against it.

6. **Language Shapes Thought**: The language someone thinks in affects how they think.

7. **Migration Changes People**: Moving to a new culture forces adaptation.

8. **Family Culture Is Primary**: The family you grow up in is your first culture.

9. **Historical Events Mark Generations**: Wars, recessions, pandemics shape everyone who lives through them.

10. **Subcultures Exist Within Cultures**: Goth, jock, nerd, punk — identities that cut across categories.

11. **Cultural Norms Change**: What was normal in 1950 isn't normal now.

12. **Multicultural People Bridge Worlds**: Those between cultures see things monocultural people don't.

13. **Culture Is Both Constraint And Resource**: It limits options but provides tools and meaning.

14. **The Past Isn't Another Country — It's A Different World**: People in different eras thought differently.

15. **Culture Is Often Unconscious**: People don't notice their own culture until they encounter others.

## How Rules Are Used

These rules aren't passive — they're **actively embedded** into the system:

1. **Prompt Builder** (`generators/prompt_builder.py`) — Rules are included in generation prompts to ensure the LLM follows them when creating persona content

2. **Chat Rules** (in `ChatContextBuilder._format_chat_rules()`) — Selected rules inform how the persona behaves during conversation

3. **Validation** — Generated content is implicitly validated against these rules through the prompt context

4. **Consistency Ledger** (`core/consistency_ledger.py`) — Cross-references ensure generated content follows the specificity rule, 3-name rule, and other mechanical rules

The rules serve as a **philosophical foundation** for the entire project. Every system, every engine, every prompt template is designed with these rules in mind. They capture the fundamental truths about human nature that make the simulation convincing.
